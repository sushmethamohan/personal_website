## About Me

I am a biological researcher with expertise in neuroscience and oncology, driven by a deep curiosity about the molecular mechanisms that control how cells grow, differentiate, and — when those controls fail — become tumors.

My scientific journey began in India, where I studied biotechnology at SASTRA University and first got a taste of research through work on nanoparticles and microbiology. That curiosity took me to Harvard-MIT, where I worked on engineering supramolecular nanoparticles for immune modulation in cancer. It was there I became truly captivated by the question of how we could intervene at the molecular level to stop tumor growth.

I went on to complete my **Ph.D. in Neuroscience at McGill University**, Montreal, under the supervision of Dr. Frederic Charron. My doctoral research revealed an unexpected role for the **nonsense-mediated mRNA decay (NMD)** pathway — a cellular quality-control system — as a tumor suppressor in medulloblastoma, the most common malignant pediatric brain tumor. By combining mouse genetics, transcriptomics, and molecular biology, I showed that NMD modulates Sonic hedgehog signaling to limit tumor aggressiveness. This work is currently in revision at *Developmental Cell* and represents the most meaningful scientific contribution of my career so far.

After my PhD, I joined the **Montreal Clinical Research Institute (IRCM)** as a post-doctoral researcher, continuing to probe RNA surveillance mechanisms in pediatric brain tumors using advanced in vivo mouse models. I then transitioned to industry at **ImmuniT**, a Montreal-based biotech, where I progressed from Scientific Analyst to **Study Director**, leading GLP-compliant immunological platform development, pre-clinical study management, and regulatory-ready reporting.

I bring a rare combination: the mechanistic curiosity and rigour of an academic scientist, and the operational discipline and regulatory awareness of an industry professional. I thrive in collaborative, fast-paced environments where careful science and creative thinking go hand in hand.

I am now **located in Germany** and am actively looking for research positions in life sciences — particularly in neuroscience, cancer biology, RNA biology, or translational oncology — where I can continue to do work that matters.

Outside the lab, I care deeply about mentoring. I have trained and supervised multiple undergraduate and graduate research trainees, and have been a biology mentor at iB Hubs in India for over five years.
