# People & Passions

### Mentoring and Collaboration

In the past, I mentored biology students at iB Hubs in India for over five years, and also tutored high school students in biology, math, and chemistry. I care about mental health in labs and about mentoring, and it's where I've actually tried to put that into practice. Teaching high schoolers, and later supervising undergrads during my PhD, showed me how much a supportive environment shapes whether someone sticks with science. It's something I carry into every lab or scientific workspace I'm part of.

Over the years, I've worked alongside collaborators across sectors and disciplines. During my PhD, I worked closely with bioinformaticians on RNA-seq analysis, shaping the questions and interpreting results together. I'm now building that computational skill myself, in R and Python, so I can carry more of that analysis end-to-end, independently. I also contributed to other projects in the lab, collaborating with senior colleagues on work that made it into publication. At ImmuniT, I collaborated with sponsor companies across India, China, Canada, and Europe, working toward pre-approval from regulatory bodies (FDA, EMA, and equivalents) for their investigational new drugs.

### Beyond the Lab

Outside the lab, I sing and teach Indian classical vocal music, love reading books, and play tennis with more enthusiasm than skill. My professional skills are considerably better, I promise.

If you'd like to talk more about cancer biology, RNA biology, neuroscience, or you're navigating something similar yourself, feel free to reach out.
